On-chain anchor · Solana devnet

A hash chain is tamper-evident to whoever holds the log — but a judge still has to trust the log in front of them is the one produced live. Anchoring closes that gap: commit the chain head on-chain, and the whole history is checkable from a single transaction signature.

How the anchor works

The oracle commits the ledger's rolling chain head — one SHA-256 that transitively commits to the entire settlement history — into a Solana Memo Program transaction. It anchors the head, not every receipt, the same way TxODDS itself anchors a Merkle root rather than every data event: equivalent tamper-evidence at a fraction of the cost.

The memo is exactly:

settlement-oracle:v1:<chain-head-sha256>

To dispute a settlement, anyone re-fetches the transaction, decodes the memo, and checks it commits to the head their receipt chains up to. No access to our server or database required.

Verification — six layers, trusting nothing

The first three layers prove a receipt is internally honest. A determined forger can fake all three at once — so the last three bind the receipt to things the forger doesn't control.

Evidence integrity

Re-hash the embedded result bundle. Catches a doctored evidence body.

Settlement replay

Re-derive the result and re-run the deterministic rule. Catches a wrong or invented YES/NO.

Receipt fingerprint

Recompute the receipt hash. Catches tampering with any bound field.

Audit chain

Recompute the hash chain and require it to contain the receipt. Catches deletion, reordering, insertion.

On-chain anchor

The Solana memo must commit to the ledger head. Catches rewriting history after it was anchored.

Source re-verify

Re-fetch the result live from TxLINE. Catches a fabricated-but-consistent bundle. (Dispute mode.)

The pipeline

01 · RESULT
game_finalised

Take the authoritative full-time result from the TxLINE scores feed — the explicit "this is final" signal, ignoring transient VAR states.

02 · SETTLE
Deterministic rule

Resolve each market as a pure function of (contract, result). Same inputs, same YES/NO, replayable by anyone.

03 · CHAIN
SHA-256 ledger

Append every settlement as a hash-chained link. Rewrite any past receipt and every downstream hash breaks.

04 · ANCHOR
Solana devnet

Commit the chain head in a Memo transaction, so the whole history is checkable from one signature.
